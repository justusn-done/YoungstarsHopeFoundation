We're a non-profit based in Kenya working to empower African youth through education, talent development, STEM engagement and community outreach — helping young people build the skills and confidence to shape their own futures.

Our programs run primarily in Kitui and Nairobi, Kenya, with community outreach events extending to surrounding areas. As we grow, we plan to expand to more regions across Africa.

Yes. YoungStars Hope Foundation-Africa is a registered non-profit organization operating in Kenya. Details on our registration are available on request — contact us if you'd like documentation for a partnership or grant application.

Four core areas: Academic Training, Talent Development, STEM Engagement, and Community Outreach. Each is designed around building practical, lasting skills rather than one-off events. See the Programs page for a full breakdown.

Our programs are open to school-age youth in the communities we serve, with a few programs extending to young adults. Specific age ranges and enrollment windows vary by program — contact us to check what's currently open.

No. All our programs are free for participants, funded through donations, grants and partnerships — which is exactly why donor and volunteer support matters so much.

Fill out the application on our Get Involved page — tell us your area of interest and availability, and a member of our team will reach out to arrange next steps, usually within a few days.

Some roles — like mentoring, content support, or grant writing — can be done remotely. Mention this in the "Tell us about yourself" field on the application form and we'll let you know what's available.
